<p><p>The livestock feed binder market consists of various types of binders that are used in animal feed formulations. These include clay, plant gums and starches, lignosulphonates, gelatin, molasses, wheat gluten, and others. Clay binders are natural minerals that improve feed efficiency and prevent feed separation. Plant gums and starches are derived from plant sources and act as binding agents. Lignosulphonates are byproducts of the paper industry and enhance pellet quality. Gelatin is a protein binder derived from animal collagen. Molasses provides energy and improves palatability. Wheat gluten is a protein-based binder. Other binders include synthetic polymers and animal-based proteins.</p></p>
