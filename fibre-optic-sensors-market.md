<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Fibre optic sensors are categorized into two types: intrinsic sensors and extrinsic sensors. Intrinsic sensors rely on the properties of the fibre optic material itself to measure and detect changes in temperature, pressure, strain, or other physical quantities. Extrinsic sensors, on the other hand, use additional components like transducers to translate the external physical changes into measurable properties of the fibre optic cables. Both types of fibre optic sensors find applications in various industries such as oil and gas, aerospace, healthcare, and telecommunications, enabling accurate and efficient monitoring and control systems.</p></p>
