<p><p>Fatigue sensing wearables in the automotive market are available in two types: physiological measurement and brainwave-based measurement. Physiological measurement wearables track indicators like heart rate, respiration, and eye movement to detect signs of fatigue in drivers. On the other hand, brainwave-based measurement wearables use electroencephalography (EEG) technology to monitor brain activity patterns and identify signs of drowsiness or distraction. Both types aim to enhance road safety by alerting drivers or triggering preventive actions when fatigue is detected.</p></p>
