<p><p>The antimicrobial paint and coating additives market can be categorized into two types: inorganic and organic. </p><p>Inorganic additives are derived from minerals and typically consist of metal ions such as silver, copper, zinc, or titanium. These additives possess strong antimicrobial properties and are effective against a broad spectrum of bacteria and fungi. They are commonly used in industries such as healthcare and food processing. </p><p>On the other hand, organic additives are derived from natural sources or synthesized chemically. They mainly include organic compounds such as quaternary ammonium salts, phenols, or pyridine derivatives. These additives also exhibit potent antimicrobial effects and are widely used in various applications like paints, coatings, and textiles.</p></p>
