<p><p>Oilfield surfactants are chemical compounds used in the oil and gas industry to improve oil recovery. There are four main types of surfactants used in this market. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge and are commonly used due to their high cleaning and emulsifying properties. Non-ionic surfactants do not have a charge and are preferred for their low foaming characteristics. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are effective in breaking down solids. Amphoteric surfactants can function as both anionic and cationic surfactants, making them versatile in different oilfield conditions.</p></p>
