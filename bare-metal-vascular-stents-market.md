<p><p>Bare-metal vascular stents are medical devices used to treat coronary artery disease by providing support to narrowed or blocked blood vessels. These stents, typically made of metals like stainless steel or cobalt-chromium alloy, are introduced into the affected vessels through minimally invasive procedures, such as percutaneous transluminal coronary angioplasty (PTCA). Once deployed, the stent helps to keep the vessel open, maintaining adequate blood flow.</p><p>The market analysis of bare-metal vascular stents indicates a steady growth rate. Factors driving the market growth include the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, increasing geriatric population, and technological advancements in stent design. However, the market growth may be hindered by the availability of alternative treatment options such as drug-eluting stents and the potential risk of restenosis (re-narrowing of the vessel) associated with bare-metal stents.</p><p>In terms of the latest trends, there is an increasing focus on the development of bioresorbable stents that can gradually dissolve within the body over time. These stents eliminate the long-term presence of a permanent metallic implant, potentially reducing the risk of future complications. Additionally, advancements in stent materials, such as the introduction of everolimus-eluting stents, aim to improve clinical outcomes and reduce the need for repeat revascularization procedures.</p><p>Overall, the bare-metal vascular stents market is expected to exhibit a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4% during the forecast period.</p></p>
