<p><ul><li>Chunky Shapes</li><li>Bow-tie or Butterfly Shapes</li><li>Shells Shapes</li><li>Quill or Pen Nib Shapes</li><li>Tubes Shapes</li><li>Flaky</li><li>Spiral Shapes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dried pasta market offers various types of shapes to cater to different consumer preferences. Chunky shapes are pasta varieties with irregular and uneven shapes, providing a hearty and rustic feel to dishes. Bow-tie or butterfly shapes are delicately crafted pieces resembling the flaps of a butterfly, adding elegance to meals. Shell shapes, as the name suggests, are pasta forms with a hollow center, allowing them to hold sauces and ingredients effectively. Quill or pen nib shapes are long, cylindrical pasta pieces known for their ability to capture and hold thinner sauces well. Tube shapes are cylindrical with an open end, making them suitable for stuffing. Flaky shapes are pasta types with uneven edges, offering a unique texture. Lastly, spiral shapes are pasta varieties twisted into a spiral-shaped design, enhancing the visual appeal and texture of dishes.</p></p>
