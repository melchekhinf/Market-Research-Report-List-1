<p><p>Ion-exchange chromatography is a separation technique used in biochemistry and analytical chemistry to separate and purify charged molecules, such as proteins, nucleic acids, and carbohydrates, based on their charge properties. It uses a solid stationary phase containing charged functional groups, which interact with and retain the target molecules, while allowing other molecules to pass through. By introducing a mobile phase with different ion concentrations or pH, molecules of interest can be eluted, resulting in the separation of the sample.</p><p>The ion-exchange chromatography market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years due to several factors. Additionally, advancements in chromatography resins and the development of novel ion-exchange materials are further contributing to market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, governments and regulatory bodies are emphasizing the importance of quality control in drug manufacturing, which requires efficient separation techniques like ion-exchange chromatography.
