<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The cellulose-based nonwoven materials market encompasses three major types: spunlace, needle-punched, and meltblown methods. Spunlace is a process where high-pressure water is used to entangle cellulose fibers for enhanced strength and smoothness. Needle-punched involves puncturing and interlocking fibers with barbed needles for increased durability. Meltblowing utilizes heated air to blow molten cellulose polymers into fine fibers that are then dispersed onto a conveyor belt. These methods are employed to create nonwoven materials with diverse applications, such as wipes, filtration, medical products, and hygiene products.</p></p>
