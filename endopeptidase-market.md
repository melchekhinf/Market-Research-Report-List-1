<p><p>Endopeptidase market types include pepsin, trypsin, chymotrypsin, and elastase. Pepsin is a digestive enzyme found in the stomach, breaking down proteins into smaller peptides. Trypsin is produced in the pancreas, aiding in protein digestion in the small intestine. Chymotrypsin also assists in protein digestion and is found in the pancreas. Elastase, produced by pancreatic cells, helps break down elastin, a protein in connective tissues. These enzymes have various applications in industries such as food processing, pharmaceuticals, and research, due to their ability to cleave peptide bonds.</p></p>
