<p><p>The In Vivo Imagers market encompasses various types of imaging technologies used for non-invasively visualizing and analyzing biological processes within living organisms. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) uses powerful magnets to produce detailed images of soft tissues. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) detects radiation emitted by injected radioactive substances to create images of metabolic activity. Micro Computed Tomography (micro-CT) is used for 3D visualization of small animal structures. Optical Imaging uses light to provide real-time images of molecular activities. Other types may include ultrasound, nuclear imaging, and near-infrared imaging.</p></p>
