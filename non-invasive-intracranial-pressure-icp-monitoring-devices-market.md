<p><p>Non-invasive intracranial pressure (ICP) monitoring devices are used to measure the pressure inside the skull without requiring invasive procedures. There are two types of devices: sound signal detection and blood flow signal detection. Sound signal detection devices use sound waves to measure changes in skull shape and pressure. Blood flow signal detection devices analyze changes in blood flow velocity and volume in the brain to determine ICP. Both types provide accurate and reliable ICP measurements while minimizing discomfort and risk for patients compared to invasive methods.</p></p>
