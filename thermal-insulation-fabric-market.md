<p><p>The thermal insulation fabric market consists of two main types, namely glass fiber and mineral wool. Glass fiber is a commonly used material, made by spinning molten glass into fine fibers, which are then woven into fabrics. This type of fabric offers excellent thermal insulation properties and is often used in applications such as HVAC systems and automotive insulation. On the other hand, mineral wool is a more environmentally friendly option, made from natural minerals like rock or slag. It provides effective thermal insulation and is commonly used in buildings and industrial settings.</p></p>
