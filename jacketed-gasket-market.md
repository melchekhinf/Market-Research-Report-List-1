<p><p>A jacketed gasket is a type of sealing material used in industries where high pressure and temperature conditions are present. It consists of a metallic outer shell that encloses a soft filler material. The metal jacket provides added strength and protection, while the soft filler material ensures effective sealing.</p><p>Market Analysis:</p><p>The jacketed gasket market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing demand from various end-use industries such as oil and gas, chemical, power generation, and petrochemicals. The rising need for effective sealing solutions in these industries to prevent leakage and ensure safety drives the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, strict government regulations regarding safety and environmental protection further contribute to the market expansion. Jacketed gaskets offer excellent resistance to high pressures and temperatures, making them suitable for applications in these industries.</p><p>Latest Trends:</p><p>1. Growing Adoption of Thermiculite-Metal Jackets: Thermiculite is a high-temperature material that provides exceptional sealing performance. <p><p>The Jacketed Gasket market consists of three types: Semi-Metallic Material, Non-Metallic Material, and Metallic Material. Semi-Metallic Material gaskets are composed of a combination of metal and non-metal materials, providing excellent sealing properties. Non-Metallic Material gaskets are made from non-metal materials like rubber or graphite, suitable for lower pressure and temperature applications. Metallic Material gaskets are entirely made from metals, offering superior strength and durability for high-pressure and high-temperature environments. These different gasket types cater to varying sealing requirements across industries.</p></p>
