<p><p>Flash chromatography is a technique used to separate and purify different compounds present in a mixture. The market for flash chromatography is categorized into various types based on the mechanism of separation. Reverse phase flash chromatography separates compounds based on their hydrophobicity or polarity. Ion exchange flash chromatography separates compounds based on their charge. Size exclusion flash chromatography separates compounds based on their molecular size. Chiral separation flash chromatography separates compounds based on their stereochemistry. Other types include normal phase, affinity, and adsorption chromatography. These techniques find applications in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, and environmental analysis.</p></p>
