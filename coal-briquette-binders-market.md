<p><p>Coal briquette binders are substances used to hold coal particles together during the briquetting process. There are three types of binders: organic, inorganic, and compound. Organic binders are derived from natural materials such as starch, lignin, and molasses. Inorganic binders include minerals like clay and lime. Compound binders are a combination of organic and inorganic materials. Each binder type offers unique characteristics, such as moisture resistance or high heat resistance, which can influence the final product's quality. Understanding these binder types is essential for manufacturers to choose the most suitable option for their coal briquette production.</p></p>
