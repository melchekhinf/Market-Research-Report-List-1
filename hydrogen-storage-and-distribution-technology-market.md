<p><p>Hydrogen storage and distribution technologies are categorized into four main types. The compressed gas market involves storing hydrogen at high pressure in tanks, allowing for easy transportation. Cold compressed liquid hydrogen involves cooling the gas to extremely low temperatures, increasing its density and enabling more hydrogen to be stored. Solid compound hydrogen storage and distribution use chemical compounds that release hydrogen when heated. Organic liquid hydrogen storage and distribution use organic molecules to absorb and desorb hydrogen, providing a compact and efficient storage solution. These technologies play a crucial role in the efficient and effective distribution of hydrogen as an energy source.</p></p>
