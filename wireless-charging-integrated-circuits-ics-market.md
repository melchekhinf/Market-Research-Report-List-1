<p><p>The Wireless Charging Integrated Circuits (ICs) market consists of two primary types of ICs: Transmitter ICs and Receiver ICs. Transmitter ICs are responsible for generating the necessary power to transmit the charging signals wirelessly. They convert the supplied power from the power source into a high-frequency alternating current (AC) waveform that can be transmitted wirelessly. On the other hand, Receiver ICs are used in devices that are being wirelessly charged. They convert the received wireless signals into usable power to charge the device's battery. Both types of ICs play a crucial role in enabling wireless charging technology.</p></p>
