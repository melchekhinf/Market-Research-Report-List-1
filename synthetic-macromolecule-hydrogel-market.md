<p><p>Synthetic macromolecule hydrogels can be classified into two main types: physical hydrogels and chemical hydrogels. Physical hydrogels are formed through physical interactions, such as hydrogen bonding or physical entanglement, and exhibit reversible gelation properties. Chemical hydrogels, on the other hand, are formed through covalent bond formation or crosslinking reactions, resulting in irreversible gelation. Both types have diverse applications in biomedical and pharmaceutical industries, including drug delivery, tissue engineering, wound healing, and sensors.</p></p>
