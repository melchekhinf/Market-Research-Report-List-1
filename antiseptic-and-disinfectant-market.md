<p><p>Antiseptics and disinfectants are chemical substances used to kill or inhibit the growth of microorganisms, such as bacteria, viruses, and fungi, on surfaces or living tissues. While both serve the purpose of preventing infections, antiseptics are typically applied to living tissues (e.g., cuts, wounds) and disinfectants are used on inanimate objects (e.g., countertops, medical instruments).</p><p>The global antiseptic and disinfectant market has been witnessing steady growth over the years, primarily driven by the increasing prevalence of infectious diseases, growing awareness regarding hygiene and sanitation, and rising demand from the healthcare industry. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has significantly boosted the demand for antiseptics and disinfectants due to the need for stringent infection control measures.</p><p>Factors such as a rise in surgical procedures, growing healthcare infrastructure, increasing disposable income, and the adoption of advanced disinfection technologies are contributing to the market's expansion. Furthermore, the focus on developing effective and safe disinfectants with minimal side effects has led to advancements in product formulations and innovations.</p><p>In terms of the latest trends, there has been a shift towards eco-friendly and sustainable disinfection solutions, with the market witnessing the introduction of products made from natural ingredients.
