These nanoparticles exhibit unique physical and chemical properties compared to their bulk counterparts, which make them ideal for a wide range of applications across various industries. The increasing demand for metal oxide nanoparticles in sectors such as electronics and energy storage is a major driving factor for market growth. Metal oxide nanoparticles are used in the production of high-performance electronics, such as transistors and semiconductors, due to their unique electrical properties.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing adoption of metal oxide nanoparticles in various medical applications, such as drug delivery systems, diagnostic imaging, and cancer treatment, is anticipated to propel market growth.
