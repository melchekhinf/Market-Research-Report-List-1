<p><p>Bioadhesives are a type of glue or adhesive derived from natural sources. There are two main types of bioadhesive markets: plant-based and animal-based. Plant-based bioadhesives are derived from renewable plant sources such as starch, cellulose, or natural oils. They are environmentally friendly and biodegradable. Animal-based bioadhesives are derived from animal proteins like collagen or gelatin. They offer good adhesive properties and are often used in medical or pharmaceutical applications. Both types of bioadhesives provide sustainable alternatives to traditional synthetic adhesives, promoting a greener and more sustainable adhesive industry.</p></p>
