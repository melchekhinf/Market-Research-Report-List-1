<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Wireless chargers for electric vehicles (EVs) can be categorized into three types: electromagnetic induction, magnetic resonance, and magneto-dynamic coupling. Electromagnetic induction uses a coil to generate an electromagnetic field, which induces a current in a receiving coil on the EV, enabling wireless charging. Magnetic resonance utilizes resonant coils to transfer energy between the charger and the EV. Magneto-dynamic coupling involves a moving part in the charger that generates a magnetic field, which is then coupled with a receiving coil on the EV, allowing for wireless charging.</p></p>
