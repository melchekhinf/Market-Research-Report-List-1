<p><p>Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride (uPVC) is a popular material used in various industries. The market for uPVC is divided into two main types: Modified uPVC and Un-modified uPVC. </p><p>Modified uPVC refers to the uPVC material that has been chemically altered to improve its properties. This modification typically involves additives to enhance flexibility, impact resistance, weatherability, and flame retardancy. It is commonly used in applications where higher performance is required, such as in window frames, pipes, and electrical cables.</p><p>On the other hand, Un-modified uPVC refers to the base uPVC material without any chemical modifications. It still exhibits good rigidity and strength, making it suitable for applications that do not require additional enhancements, such as sewer pipes, water supply pipes, and various building materials.</p><p>Both Modified and Un-modified uPVC have their own market segments, catering to different industries and applications based on their specific needs and requirements.</p></p>
